Why KPI Analysis Matters

KPIs help turn raw numbers into useful decisions

KPIs are important because they highlight the metrics that actually matter instead of making users scan every column and every value in the dataset.

KPI analysis is important because it converts raw cleaned data into focused business or project questions such as performance, growth, risk, quality, or efficiency.

When the right KPIs are selected, the final dashboard becomes easier to understand, easier to present, and much more valuable for decision-making.
